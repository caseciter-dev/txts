RELEVANT STATUTORY FRAMEWORK 
 
13. Before we examine the propriety of the course 
adopted by the High Court, it is necessary to 
understand the statutory setting in which the dispute 
arose. The controversy does not concern appointment 
to the post of Assistant Commandant, General Duty, 
in isolation. It concerns the point at which officers 
drawn from different sources of recruitment are to be 
placed in the same seniority list. The relevant 
comparison is between the 43rd batch of Directly 
Appointed Gazetted Officers, whose recruitment 
process com menced pursuant to the notification 
dated 09.05.2009, and the local promotees who claim 
placement on the basis of the Supplementary 
Departmental Promotion Committee held on 
20.10.2010. 
14. The 2001 Rules governed the field when the 
direct recruitment process pursuant to the 
notification dated 09.05.2009 was initiated. Rule 8 of 
the 2001 Rules dealt with seniority. Sub -rule (1) 
provided that all officers holding a higher rank would 

--- Page 18 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  14 
 
be senior to officers holding a lower rank. Sub -rule 
(2) provided that, in a particular rank, seniority of 
officers appointed to any post would be determined 
in accordance with the order of selection for 
appointment to that post. Sub-rule (3) then dealt with 
inter se seniority amongst officers. In that context, 
the proviso to Rule 8(3) of the 2001 Rules provided as 
follows:  
“Provided that in case of direct entrants, 
the year of declaration of results shall be 
the year of seniority. Inter -se seniority of 
the directly recruited ACs and promotees 
will be decided as per orders of DOP&T 
issued from time to time.” 
 
15. During the pendency of the direct recruitment 
process and before the declaration of the final result, 
the 2010 Rules came into force. Rule 6 of the 2010 
Rules deals with seniority. Sub-rule (1) continues the 
general principle that officers holding a higher  rank 
shall be senior to officers holding a lower rank. Sub-
rule (2) provides that, in a particular rank, seniority 
of officers appointed to any post shall be determined 
in accordance with the order of selection for 
appointment to the post. Sub-rule (3) then addresses 
inter se seniority amongst officers holding the same 

--- Page 19 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  15 
 
rank. It also refers to the Central Reserve Police Force 
Standing Order No. 1 of 2009 dated 12.01.2009. The 
proviso to Rule 6(3), which is at the centre of the 
present controversy, reads as follows: 
 
“Provided that the seniority of directly 
appointed gazetted officers and direct 
entry gazetted officers shall be reckoned 
from the date of declaration of results and 
that of the local promotees from the date of 
departmental promotion committee by 
whom they were empanelled.” 
 
16. The rival claims in the present case arise 
because the direct recruitment process commenced 
when the 2001 Rules were in force, whereas the 
UPSC result, the Supplementary Departmental 
Promotion Committee, and the subsequent 
placement exercise took place aft er the 2010 Rules 
had come into force. The directly recruited officers 
contend that the proviso to Rule 6(3) of the 2010 
Rules furnishes a clear answer, since their UPSC 
result was declared before the Supplementary 
Departmental Promotion Committee dated 
20.10.2010. The local promotees contend that the 
matter cannot be reduced to a ministerial correction 
of one date in the record. According to them, the High 

--- Page 20 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  16 
 
Court was required to examine the transition from 
the 2001 Rules to the 2010 Rules, the effect of the 
Department of Personnel and Training instructions 
referred to in the 2001 Rules, the principle governing 
placement of direct recruits and promotees in a 
common seniority list, the effect of the seniority 
position having remained undisturbed for several 
years, and the rights of all affected officers. The issue, 
therefore, is not only whether the date of declaration 
of the UPSC result was 12 /13.10.2010 or 
01.06.2011. The real issue is whether the seniority 
position could be altered in the manner in which it 
was altered, without a full adjudication of the 
applicable Rules and the competing claims arising 
under them. 
 
ANALYSIS 
 
17. In our considered opinion, the manner in which 
W.P.(C) No. 14092 of 2021 came to be disposed of 
requires close attention. The order dated 18.11.2022, 
as originally passed, was in the nature of a direction 
to the official respondents to take a final decision in 
accordance with law. Such an order did not, by itself, 
purport to finally decide the inter se seniority 

--- Page 21 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  17 
 
between the directly recruited officers and the local 
promotees.  
18. However, by the subsequent order dated 
23.11.2022, paragraph 1 of the order dated 
18.11.2022 was modified. The modified paragraph 
recorded that the result of the UPSC examination for 
the 43rd batch of Directly Appointed Gazetted 
Officers had been declared before the convening of 
the Supplementary Departmental Promotion 
Committee dated 20.10.2010, and that, therefore, the 
said officers were eligible to be assigned seniority 
above the local promotees. This was not a mere 
clerical correction. It introduced int o the order a 
substantive premise touching the very basis on which 
inter se seniority was to be determined.  
19. A direction to an administrative authority to 
consider and decide a claim in accordance with law 
cannot be equated with an adjudication of the legal 
basis of that claim. Once the High Court recorded a 
premise which substantially indicated how the 
seniority dispute was to be resolved, the subsequent 
administrative exercise could not remain unaffected 
by that premise. The order dated 24.03.2023 issued 

--- Page 22 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  18 
 
by the CRPF was passed in that background and 
resulted in the revision of the seniority position.  
20. The order dated 24.03.2023 treated 
12/13.10.2010 as the date of declaration of the result 
of the 43rd batch of Directly Appointed Gazetted 
Officers instead of 01.06.2011, which had earlier 
been treated as the relevant date on the basis of the 
Unofficial Note of the Ministry of Home Affairs. The 
consequence was that the 43rd batch of Directly 
Appointed Gazetted Officers was placed above the 
local promotees whose claim was referable to the 
Supplementary Departmental Promotion Committee 
dated 20.10.2010. Therefore, the said order was not 
an isolated administrative correction. It altered the 
placement of two streams of officers in the common 
seniority list.   
21. The connected writ petition, namely W.P.(C) No. 
10777 of 2023, was a direct challenge to the order 
dated 24.03.2023. While dismissing that writ petition 
by judgment dated 16.08.2023, the High Court 
upheld the revised placement of the directly recruited 
officers above the local promotees. The result was 
that the first writ petition led to the administrative 
order dated 24.03.2023, and the second writ petition 

--- Page 23 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  19 
 
upheld that very order. In such a situation, the two 
writ petitions are not independent proceedings in any 
real sense. They form part of one integrated seniority 
dispute and ought to receive one coherent 
adjudication.    
22. The record also indicates that the controversy 
was not confined only to the petitioners before the 
High Court in the first writ petition. Several officers 
from both streams were likely to be affected by any 
alteration of the seniority position. Seniority i n a 
disciplined force is not merely a matter of placement 
in a list. It bears directly upon further promotion, 
command structure, and service progression. The 
participation of all necessary and proper parties, 
therefore, assumes significance. The applications for 
impleadment before this Court also arose because of 
this concern.  
23. The legal issues also require a fuller 
examination than what was undertaken in the 
proceedings from which the present appeals arise. 
The promotees rely upon the commencement of the 
recruitment process under the 2001 Rules, the 
reference in the 2001 Rules to the instructions issued 
by the Department of Personnel and Training, the 

--- Page 24 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  20 
 
effect of the earlier seniority position, and the 
principles governing inter se placement of promotees 
and direct recruits. The directly recruited officers rely 
upon the express language of Rule 6(3) of the 2010 
Rules and submit that the date of declaratio n of 
result is decisive. These rival positions required a 
complete adjudication with reference to the 
applicable Rules, the relevant Office Memoranda, and 
the effect of the transition from the 2001 Rules to the 
2010 Rules.  
24. The decisions of this Court in Union of India v. 
N.R. Parmar 5 and K. Meghachandra Singh v. 
Ningam Siro 6 have also been relied upon by the 
respective parties. The direct recruits and the Union 
seek to distinguish K. Meghachandra Singh (supra) 
by contending that the present case is governed by a 
specific statutory rule. The promotees, on the other 
hand, rely upon the principle that seniority cannot 
ordinarily be assigned from a date when an officer 
had not entered the cadre. These submissions cannot 
be answered in the abstract. They have to be 
 
5 (2012) 13 SCC 340 
6 (2020) 5 SCC 689 

--- Page 25 ---
C.A. NOS.4585-4586 OF 2025  21 
 
considered in the setting of the CRPF Rules and the 
facts of the present case.  
25. It has also been brought to our notice that the 
correctness and scope of the principles flowing from 
K. Meghachandra Singh (supra), in relation to inter 
se seniority between direct recruits and promotees, is 
pending consideration before a Constitution Bench 
pursuant to the reference in Hariharan v. Harsh 
Vardhan Singh Rao7. We do not treat the pendency 
of the said reference as a bar to adjudication. Nor do 
we express any opinion on its effect on the present 
controversy. The reference is noticed only to indicate 
that the legal questions raised before us are 
substantial and s hould be considered by the High 
Court in their proper factual and statutory context.  
26. In these circumstances, any final 
pronouncement by this Court on the merits of the 
rival claims may prejudice one side or the other 
without the benefit of a full adjudication by the High 
Court in both writ petitions together. The better 
course is to ensure that the High Court examines the 
entire controversy afresh, including the legality and 
effect of the order dated 24.03.2023, after hearing all

necessary parties and after considering the 
applicable statutory Rules, Office Memoranda, and 
precedents.  
27. There is one further aspect which relates to the 
working of the cadre during the pendency of the 
remanded proceedings. The interim orders passed in 
these matters have affected further promotions. 
During the course of hearing, it was submitted on 
behalf of the Union of India  that there are sufficient 
vacancies available and that the Union of India may 
be permitted to make promotions provisionally, 
subject to the final outcome. Since the ultimate 
seniority position would abide by the decision to be 
rendered by the High Court, the interest of service 
administration can be protected by permitting 
provisional promotions of eligible officers from both 
streams, subject to the final result of the writ 
petitions.
