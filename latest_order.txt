1. Delay Condoned. Leave Granted. 
2. Having regard to the nature of the order impugned as
also the nature of the order that we propose to pass,
Mr.  Siddhartha  Iyer,  learned  counsel  for  the
respondent,  who  has  accepted  notice  for  the
respondent,  does  not  propose  to  file  a  counter
affidavit and has agreed to final disposal of the
appeal at this stage itself.
3. Heard learned counsel for the parties.
4. This appeal arises from an order of the High Court of
Karnataka at Bengaluru dated 05.03.2026, whereby I.A.
No.1/2024 in RFA No.2093/2024 seeking condonation of
delay of 323 days in filing regular first appeal has
1

--- Page 13 ---
been rejected and, consequently, the appeal has been
dismissed as barred by limitation.  
5. The short submission on behalf of the appellant is
that there is no consideration of the explanation
offered in the delay condonation application by the
High Court and, therefore, the matter requires a fresh
consideration.
6. The learned counsel for the respondent submits that
there may be no appropriate explanation offered for
condonation of delay and, therefore, the Court may not
have  dealt  with  the  reasons/explanation  if  any
offered.  
7. Be that as it may, since we find that the High Court
has  not  discussed  the  explanation  at  all  while
rejecting the delay condonation application, in our
view, the matter requires a fresh consideration by the
High Court.
8. Accordingly, this appeal is allowed. The impugned
order dated 05.03.2026 is set aside. I.A. No.1/2024 in
RFA No.2093/2024 shall be restored on the file of the
High Court for a fresh consideration and decision in
accordance with the law after giving opportunity of
2

hearing to both sides.
9. It is expected that the High Court shall accord due
consideration to the application and decide the same
expeditiously preferably within a period of a month
from the date of production of a certified copy of
this order.
10. It is made clear that we have not expressed any
opinion as regards the merits of the delay condonation
application.
11. Pending application(s), if any, shall stand disposed
of.
