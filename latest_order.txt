--- Page 9 ---
2
2. The present Special Leave Petition calls in question
the correctness of the order dated 14-05-2026 in MCRLC
No. 21184/2026 passed by the High Court of Madhya
Pradesh Principal Seat at Jabalpur. By the said order,
the High Court has declined the benefit of bail to the
petitioner in connection with FIR/Crime No.377/2025
registered at Police Station Shahganj, District Sehore,
Madhya  Pradesh,  for  the  offences  punishable  under
Section 34(2) of Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1915 (for
short, ‘the Act’).
3. Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that
taking the prosecution’s case at the highest, there is
no  conscious  possession  established  against  the
petitioner. According to the learned counsel for the
petitioner the recovery was seized from a land already
acquired by the National Highways Authority of India
and the land was not in possession of the petitioner.
4. This is, however, seriously disputed by the learned
AAG for the State. Learned AAG for the State on the
other hand submits that petitioner has an earlier
criminal antecedent. Learned AAG handed over an order
dated  22.07.2020  of  the  High  Court,  whereby  the
petitioner was enlarged on bail in the said other case.
One of the condition imposed was that the petitioner
shall not commit similar offences. The said order is
taken on record.
5. We have considered the contentions of the learned

--- Page 10 ---
3
counsel  for  the  parties  and  kept  in  mind  the
ingredients of Section 34(2) as well as Section 59-A of
the Act.
6. Considering the overall facts and circumstances of
the case and having heard the learned AAG for the State
as also keeping in mind the case of the prosecution
about the place from which the seizure is alleged to
have been made, we are satisfied that the petitioner is
entitled to be enlarged on bail. We have also kept the
earlier antecedent pointed out by the learned AAG for
the State. The petitioner has been in custody since
27.04.2026. The Trial is also likely to take some time.
The maximum sentence imposed is 03 years and the
offence is Magistrate triable.
7. Keeping in mind all the circumstances, we direct
that the petitioner shall be enlarged on bail subject
to the satisfaction of the concerned Trial Court in
connection  with  the  FIR/Crime  No.377/2025  for  the
offences mentioned hereinabove.
8. The Special Leave Petition is disposed of in the
above terms.
9. Pending application(s), if any, shall also stand
disposed of.

Present  Special  Leave  Petitions  have  been  filed
challenging the order dated 11th February 2020 by which the
Second Appeal filed by the Petitioners was dismissed for
non-prosecution as well as the Order dated 08th October 2025
whereby the High Court dismissed the application filed by
1

--- Page 15 ---
the Petitioners for condonation of delay of nearly four and
a half years in filing the restoration application of the
second appeal.
It is the case of the Petitioners that they are the
bona fide  litigants whose appeal was dismissed on 11 th
February 2020 on account of non-appearance of their counsel
which was neither intentional nor deliberate, but occurred
due to professional exigencies of the counsel who was
appearing before another  Bench.
It is further averred that immediately upon gaining
the knowledge of dismissal, the Petitioners had taken steps
to file an application for restoration. However, soon
thereafter the functioning of the High Court was disrupted
due to COVID-19 pandemic. 
It  is  also  averred  that  the  listing  of  the
application skipped the mind of the clerk attached to the
counsel for the Petitioners and this fact came to the
knowledge of the counsel during the month of October 2024
and he immediately re-filed a restoration application as
the first application was not traceable in the Registry.  
This Court finds that the entire blame for delay in
refiling the restoration application has been placed on the
clerk of the Advocate.  It is not understood that if the
restoration application had been filed immediately after
the  second  appeal  was  dismissed  on  account  of  non-
prosecution,  why  has  the  filing  number  of  the  first
restoration application not mentioned.
2

--- Page 16 ---
It is strange that even after the limitation for
filing the applications during the Covid period had come to
an end in March 2022, the counsel for the Petitioners had
not taken any steps to get his restoration application
listed or inquired into.  
Consequently, this Court is in agreement with the
view of the High Court that no sufficient cause for delay
in filing the restoration application has been made out as
the present case is of negligence, lack of bona fide and
inaction. 
Even on merits, this Court finds that the trial Court
has recorded a finding that Petitioners’ own witness (PW4)
has admitted the signature of the testator on the disputed
Will as genuine. 
Accordingly, the present Special Leave Petitions are
dismissed.
Pending application(s), if any, shall stand disposed
of.
